This website is about Space

Space, a cosmic expanse of stars and galaxies, holds the allure of the unknown. Planets orbit in rhythmic dance, while distant quasars emit captivating glows. Its vastness continues to spark human curiosity, inviting exploration into the mysteries that lie beyond our planet.

Some of the most known celestial objects

M31 is located in the constellation Andromeda and is best observed in November. Boasting an apparent magnitude of 3.1, the galaxy can be seen with the naked eye, even in areas with moderate light pollution.

Andromeda Galaxy (M31) 2 million light-years away from Earth

On a clear night, void of light pollution, we can catch a glimpse of the bright lights of the galactic city streaking across the night sky. Our window into the universe, this milky white band of stars, dust and gas is where our galaxy gets its name.

Milky Way (Our Galaxy)

With an apparent magnitude of 6, the Eagle Nebula can be spotted through a small telescope and is best viewed during July. A large telescope and optimal viewing conditions are necessary to resolve the Pillars of Creation.

Ealge Nebula (M16) 7 000 light-years away from Earth

This star-forming cloud of interstellar gas is located in the constellation Sagittarius and its apparent magnitude of 6 makes it faintly visible to the naked eye in dark skies. The best time to observe M8 is during August.
